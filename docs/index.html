U.S. County Economic Activity Map

Intro view

This map introduces the value of the tracker by showing county-level economic momentum across the United States. It uses nighttime lights as a directional economic indicator and is most useful for identifying local change, regional divergence, and early signals of growth or slowdown.

How the tracker works

Econ Light Monitor uses NASA nighttime lights to estimate shifts in economic activity over time, then layers in official BLS labor-force and employment data for the U.S. to build more interpretable economic signals and nowcasts. LAUS provides monthly employment, unemployment, labor-force, and unemployment-rate data for states, counties, metropolitan areas, and many cities. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

V2 adds a simple AI nowcast layer that translates recent light momentum into estimated current employment and labor-market direction. These are model-based estimates, not official statistics.
